Meet The Farmers!

Donnell Roots Run Deep...

In 1835, John Donnell moved his family to Tennessee to what would become the Madison County village of Spring Creek. For six generations Donnell familes have raised their children and cropped very small portions of this same Donnell land. Billy & Rose Ann (McCallum) Donnell (right phto) along with their son Andrew, his wife Jessica and children Callum, Catey Beth & Connor (left photo) live working on this real farm. These children are the 7th generation to continuously live and work on the Donnell Family Farm. Today, The Donnell Century Farm produces registered Angus cattle, soy bean, cotton, corn, and hay. It is with a hopeful heart we present a look at agriculture as it was and as it is today!

Farm Polices

PETS: Only pets assisting the handicapped are allowed at Donnell Century Farms.

SMOKING: There is to be NO smoking on the property. If we see you smoking, we will assume you are on fire and proceed to put you out!!

ALCOHOL: Alcohol and drugs are NOT permitted on our property!!

NO HORSE PLAY: Running through the maze will NOT be tolerated! Not only is it considered obnoxious but it can also dangerous.

UNATTENDED CHILDREN: Everyone unattended child will be given an espresso, a chaw of Bull Durham, AND a free puppy/kitten!!

OUTSIDE FOOD AND DRINKS: No outside food/drinks allowed on the farm. Birthday cakes/cupcakes are the only exemption.
